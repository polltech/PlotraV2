Coffee Farming Guide

Your comprehensive guide to sustainable coffee production in Kenya

Welcome to Coffee Farming

Coffee farming in Kenya is both an art and a science. With proper care and attention to detail, you can produce high-quality coffee that commands premium prices in the global market. This guide will walk you through the essential practices for successful coffee farming.

Site Selection and Preparation

1

Choose the Right Location

Coffee thrives at altitudes between 1,300-2,200 meters above sea level with well-distributed rainfall (1,200-2,000mm annually) and temperatures between 15-24°C.

Altitude: 1,300-2,200m above sea level
Rainfall: 1,200-2,000mm per year
Temperature: 15-24°C average
Well-drained, fertile soil (pH 5.5-6.5)
2

Land Preparation

Proper land preparation ensures healthy root development and optimal yields.

  • Clear vegetation: Remove weeds and competing plants
  • Soil testing: Check pH and nutrient levels
  • Terracing: Prevent soil erosion on slopes
  • Shade trees: Plant appropriate shade trees 2-3 months before coffee

Planting Coffee

Recommended Varieties for Kenya

  • Batian: High yield, disease resistant, good cup quality
  • Ruiru 11: Drought tolerant, good for marginal areas
  • K7: Traditional variety, good for blending
  • SL28 & SL34: Premium quality, disease susceptible
3

Planting Process

Follow these steps for successful coffee establishment:

  1. Nursery: Raise seedlings in shaded nursery for 6-12 months
  2. Spacing: 2m x 2m (2,500 plants per hectare)
  3. Planting time: March-May (long rains) or September-November (short rains)
  4. Depth: Plant at same depth as nursery, firm soil around roots
  5. Mulching: Apply organic mulch to retain moisture

Pest and Disease Management

Important

Early detection and proper management of pests and diseases is crucial for maintaining coffee quality and yields. Regular monitoring and Integrated Pest Management (IPM) approaches are recommended.

Common Pests

  • Coffee Berry Borer: Use pheromone traps and parasitic wasps
  • Antestia Bugs: Apply systemic insecticides during fruit development
  • Leaf Miner: Remove affected leaves and use biological control

Common Diseases

  • Coffee Berry Disease (CBD): Fungicide application, resistant varieties
  • Coffee Leaf Rust: Copper-based fungicides, proper spacing
  • Bacterial Blight: Remove infected plants, use clean tools

Harvesting and Processing

4

Harvesting

Selective harvesting ensures quality and extends the harvest season.

  • Timing: Harvest when 80% of cherries are red
  • Frequency: Pick every 7-10 days during peak season
  • Method: Hand picking for quality, strip picking for speed
  • Storage: Keep harvested cherries in shade, process within 24 hours

Processing Methods

Wet Processing (Washed)

  1. Depulp cherries immediately
  2. Ferment for 12-24 hours
  3. Wash and dry parchment
  4. Hull to obtain green beans

Dry Processing (Natural)

  1. Dry whole cherries in sun
  2. Turn regularly for even drying
  3. Hull dried cherries
  4. Clean and sort green beans

Sustainable Practices

  • Soil conservation: Use terraces and cover crops
  • Water management: Implement efficient irrigation
  • Biodiversity: Plant shade trees and maintain natural habitats
  • Integrated pest management: Minimize chemical use
